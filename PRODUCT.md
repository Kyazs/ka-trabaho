# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Out-of-school Filipino youth aged 15-24 across Philippine provinces. They are on basic smartphones with prepaid data, often low-bandwidth connections. Many are high school graduates or ALS completers who feel shut out of traditional employment paths. They encounter the app in moments of uncertainty — browsing job posts, hearing about TESDA from a neighbor, or searching "paano makapag-aral ng libre" on their phone. Their context is personal, mobile, and often shared devices.

## Product Purpose

Ka-TrabaHO connects out-of-school youth with free TESDA training programs and local job opportunities through AI-powered skills assessment. It replaces the overwhelming experience of browsing 300+ course catalogs with a guided, personalized recommendation flow. Success is measured by a user completing the assessment, finding a relevant course, and taking a concrete step toward enrollment — whether that's visiting a TESDA center, chatting with the AI counselor, or understanding the requirements for their matched course.

## Brand Personality

Encouraging, Accessible, Action-oriented.

The voice is warm without being patronizing. It meets users where they are — no jargon, no judgment, no assumption of credentials. Every screen pushes toward the next concrete action. Taglish is not a compromise; it is the natural register of the audience.

## Anti-references

- **Government portal stiffness**: The typical .gov.ph look — dense text walls, tiny fonts, outdated layouts, bureaucratic tone that makes a 17-year-old feel like they're filling out tax forms. Ka-TrabaHO must never feel like paperwork.
- **Corporate SaaS dashboard**: Slick enterprise vibes — navy-and-white tables, metric dashboards, HR-manager aesthetics. This is for youth seeking their first break, not managers tracking KPIs.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the path, not the catalog.** Don't dump 300+ courses on a user. Guide them through a personalized assessment that narrows to relevant options. The AI is the difference between a searchable database and a counselor.
2. **Speak their language, literally.** Taglish/English bilingual is not a feature toggle — it is respect for how users actually communicate. Every label, error message, and celebration must sound natural in both registers.
3. **No diploma, no problem.** Every interaction must reinforce that lack of formal credentials is not a barrier. The UI should never make a user feel underqualified — it should make them feel one step away from qualification.
4. **Phone-first, data-light.** Designed for basic smartphones on prepaid data. Every byte, tap, and scroll matters. No heavy assets, no unnecessary animations on slow connections, no layouts that break on narrow viewports.
5. **One action away from enrollment.** Always surface the next concrete step — chat with Ka-TrabaHO about requirements, visit the nearest TESDA center, check what documents to prepare. Dead ends are failures.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. The audience includes users with limited literacy, older relatives helping younger family members navigate the app, and people on shared or borrowed devices. Specific considerations:

- Bilingual interface reduces language barriers
- Clear visual hierarchy for low-literacy contexts — icons paired with text, generous touch targets (minimum 44px), no icon-only controls
- Reduced motion support already implemented
- Color is never the sole indicator of state
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible with visible focus indicators
- Chat interface supports screen readers with semantic markup
